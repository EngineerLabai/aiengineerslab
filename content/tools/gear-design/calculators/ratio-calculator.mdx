## Calculation Explanation

<Callout type="info" title="How to use?">
<StepList title="Quick steps" steps={["Enter tooth counts", "Add input speed and torque", "Apply efficiency and read outputs"]} />
</Callout>

<Formula label="Gear ratio">
i = z2 / z1
n2 = n1 / i
T2 = T1 · i · η
</Formula>

**Assumptions**
- Single gear pair with rigid teeth.
- Efficiency is represented by a single constant η.

**Units**
- z: tooth count (dimensionless)
- n: rpm
- T: N·m

**References**
- AGMA 2001 (Fundamental gear design)
- ISO 6336 (Cylindrical gears)

<Callout type="warning" title="Out of scope / Warning">
Backlash, dynamic factors, and multi-stage gear trains are not included.
</Callout>

## Examples

### Example 1: 3:1 ratio

**Inputs**
- z1 = 20, z2 = 60
- n1 = 1500 rpm
- T1 = 50 N·m
- η = 0.97

**Outputs**
- i = 3.0
- n2 = 500 rpm
- T2 = 145.5 N·m

### Example 2: 2:1 ratio

**Inputs**
- z1 = 18, z2 = 36
- n1 = 1200 rpm
- T1 = 80 N·m
- η = 0.95

**Outputs**
- i = 2.0
- n2 = 600 rpm
- T2 = 152 N·m
