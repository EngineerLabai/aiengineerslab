## Calculation Explanation

<Callout type="info" title="How to use?">
<StepList title="Quick steps" steps={["Enter module, tooth counts, and angles", "Set face width and addendum", "Read eα, eβ, and total contact ratio"]} />
</Callout>

<Formula label="Contact ratio">
m_t = m_n / cos(β)
α_t = atan(tan(α_n) / cos(β))
p_b = π · m_t · cos(α_t)
e_α = (s1 + s2 − a · sin(α_t)) / p_b
e_β = b · sin(β) / (π · m_n)
e = e_α + e_β
</Formula>

**Assumptions**
- Standard full-depth gears (ha* ≈ 1).
- No profile shift; ISO/DIN reference geometry.

**Units**
- m_n, b, a: mm
- Angles: degrees

**References**
- ISO 6336 (Cylindrical gears)
- ISO 21771 (Gear geometry definitions)

<Callout type="warning" title="Out of scope / Warning">
Profile shift, internal gears, and high correction factors are not covered. Check undercut and root strength separately.
</Callout>

## Examples

### Example 1: Helical pair

**Inputs**
- m_n = 2, z1 = 18, z2 = 54
- α_n = 20°, β = 15°
- b = 35 mm, ha* = 1

**Outputs**
- e_α = 1.573
- e_β = 1.442
- e = 3.014

### Example 2: Spur gear

**Inputs**
- m_n = 3, z1 = 20, z2 = 40
- α_n = 20°, β = 0°
- b = 30 mm, ha* = 1

**Outputs**
- e_α = 1.635
- e_β = 0.000
- e = 1.635
