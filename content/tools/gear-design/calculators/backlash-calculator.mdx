## Calculation Explanation

<Callout type="info" title="How to use?">
<StepList title="Quick steps" steps={["Enter module, face width, and center distance", "Add temperature delta and thermal expansion", "Review nominal and adjusted backlash"]} />
</Callout>

<Formula label="Backlash estimate">
j_nom = 0.04 · m + 0.001 · b
j_min = 0.8 · j_nom
j_max = 1.2 · j_nom
Δa = a · α_th · ΔT
j_adj = j + Δa
</Formula>

**Assumptions**
- Simple ISO/DIN-inspired heuristic for initial sizing.
- Thermal expansion is applied to center distance only.

**Units**
- m, b, a: mm
- α_th: µm/m·°C
- j: mm

**References**
- ISO 1328 (Accuracy of cylindrical gears)
- DIN 3967 (Backlash guidance)

## Examples

### Example 1: Mild thermal shift

**Inputs**
- m = 2, b = 30 mm
- a = 150 mm, ΔT = 10 °C
- α_th = 12 µm/m·°C

**Outputs**
- j_nom = 0.110 mm
- j_min = 0.088 mm
- j_max = 0.132 mm
- Δa = 0.018 mm
- j_nom_adj = 0.128 mm

### Example 2: Larger gear, higher ΔT

**Inputs**
- m = 3, b = 40 mm
- a = 200 mm, ΔT = 20 °C
- α_th = 12 µm/m·°C

**Outputs**
- j_nom = 0.160 mm
- j_min = 0.128 mm
- j_max = 0.192 mm
- Δa = 0.048 mm
- j_nom_adj = 0.208 mm
