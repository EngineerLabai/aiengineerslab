## Calculation Explanation

<Callout type="info" title="How to use?">
<StepList title="Quick steps" steps={["Enter pulley diameters and center distance", "Check that C > (D1 + D2)/2", "Read belt length and wrap angle"]} />
</Callout>

<Formula label="Open belt length">
L = 2C + (π/2)(D1 + D2) + (D2 − D1)^2 / (4C)
β = π − 2·arcsin((D2 − D1)/(2C))
</Formula>

**Assumptions**
- Open belt configuration (not crossed).
- Belt thickness and stretch are ignored; geometric approximation only.

**Units**
- D1, D2, C: mm
- L: mm
- β: degrees

**References**
- Machine design handbooks (belt drive geometry)

<Callout type="warning" title="Out of scope / Warning">
Timing belts, crossed belts, and tensioning allowances are not included. Add a stretch/tension allowance per manufacturer data.
</Callout>

## Examples

### Example 1: Different pulley diameters

**Inputs**
- D1 = 120 mm, D2 = 80 mm
- C = 400 mm

**Outputs**
- L = 1115.2 mm
- β = 185.7°

### Example 2: Equal pulleys

**Inputs**
- D1 = 150 mm, D2 = 150 mm
- C = 500 mm

**Outputs**
- L = 1471.2 mm
- β = 180°
